Chuck Berry

Born: October 18, 1926 Education: High School Diploma, and Poro College for Hairdressing Primarily worked for: Chess Records

Biography

Chuck Berry was an African American singer, guitarist, and songwriter. Nicknamed the "Father of Rock and Roll" he developed many major elements in music that made Rock and Roll so distinct. He was born into a middle class family in St.Louis Missouri. Gained a passion for music in High School but later stopped as he was convicted of armed robbery and sent to a reformatory. Berry later settled for a married life and worked at an auto plant. Picked up his love for music and with that traveled to Chicago in 1955 where he met Muddy Waters. He would later be introduced to the owner of Chess Records and record the song "Maybellene" which would sell a million copies. By 1950s he became an established star and began touring and releasing music with Chess Records. Berry would become the first musicians to be inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall Fame in its opening in 1986. He would live a long and successful life. Would pass away in 2019 of a cardiac arrest.
